I am a PhD candidate in Economics at Northeastern University. My research fields are labor economics and industrial organization, with a focus on how healthcare privatization and place-based tax policies reshape local labor markets.

My job market paper studies the labor market effects of Puerto Rico's 1993 healthcare reform, which replaced the island's public delivery system with privatized managed care across all 78 municipalities. Using staggered difference-in-differences methods, I document how system-wide privatization—where no public fallback remains—produces qualitatively different supply-side effects than piecemeal reforms.

I will be available for interviews at the 2027 ASSA Annual Meeting in Washington, DC.
